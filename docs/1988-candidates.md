# 1988 Mode Candidates

The songs outlined here would make good chisces for testing the system in 1988 mode, allowing one to test whether The KLF followed
their own manual.

## The context

Doctorin' the Tardis was released 23 May 1988. It hit number one for one week, from 12th to 19th June 1988. So the relevant chart weeks to look at are:

- **29 May** — the week it entered (at #22 per the chart note)
- **5 June** — climbing to #4
- **12 June** — hits #1, displacing Wet Wet Wet
- **19 June** — drops to #2, behind Bros "I Owe You Nothing"

## The shortlist for testing

The Manual's argument is essentially that *any* song could be made to work if you followed the rules. So your best test set has two layers — songs **in the chart the same week** (did the competition also follow the rules?) and songs the KLF would have specifically been aware of as the template (SAW productions, the dominant sound).

**Tier 1 — same chart, same week, directly comparable:**

- **Kylie Minogue — Got to Be Certain** (SAW, #2 that week before Tardis, #6 the week Tardis hit #1). The platonic SAW single. If anything should pass the Manual criteria it's this.
- **Bros — I Owe You Nothing (1988)** (the record that knocked Tardis off #1). Same moment, different sonic world — rock-leaning pop vs pure SAW.
- **Wet Wet Wet — With a Little Help from My Friends** (#1 the week before Tardis). A cover, charity record, very different structure — interesting as a counter-example.
- **Maxi Priest — Wild World** (charting that week, #5–7). Reggae-pop — different BPM, different groove feel entirely.

**Tier 2 — SAW productions from the same period (the template):**

- **Rick Astley — Never Gonna Give You Up** (Jan 1988 #1) — the canonical SAW sound, similar BPM range. If the tool can't identify this as ticking the boxes something is off.
- **Kylie Minogue — I Should Be So Lucky** (the *first* SAW/Kylie single, Feb 1988 #1). Shorter, simpler structure than Got to Be Certain.
- **S'Express — Theme from S'Express** (#1 in May, knocked off the week before Tardis entered). House-adjacent, very different energy shape — good stress test for the structure alignment.

**Tier 3 — deliberate contrast:**

- **Fairground Attraction — Perfect** (#1 in early May, #3 the week Tardis entered). Acoustic, no drums, 122 BPM, atypical structure. Should *fail* most Manual criteria decisively — useful for validating the tool gives low scores to things that genuinely don't fit.
- **New Order — Blue Monday 1988** (remixed for chart success, #3 that same period). 120 BPM, very long (near 7 mins on some versions — use the 7" edit), no chorus in the conventional sense. Another principled fail case.

---

That gives you about 8–9 tracks. Doctorin' the Tardis itself plus those should be enough to answer the question: does the tool confirm that the SAW singles score high on `1988` mode while the outliers (Fairground Attraction, New Order) score low? If so, the KLF's thesis holds up under measurement.

The Kylie pair (I Should Be So Lucky + Got to Be Certain) will also be interesting in themselves — they're structurally almost identical and were made to a formula, so you'd expect them to score similarly, and any large divergence would tell you something about where the analysis modules need calibrating.
